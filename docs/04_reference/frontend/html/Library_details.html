Paper Abstract

The recent advancements in Multi-modal Large Language Models (MLLMs) have shown remarkable capabilities in short video understanding. However, long-form video understanding remains a significant challenge due to the massive computational overhead and the requirement for long-term temporal reasoning. In this work, we propose a novel architectural framework that leverages a Hierarchical Spatiotemporal Bottleneck (HSB) to efficiently process extended video sequences. Our approach enables the model to maintain high-resolution spatial details while compressing temporal redundancies, allowing for contextual reasoning over videos lasting up to several minutes. We demonstrate that our model significantly outperforms existing benchmarks in long-form action segmentation and complex event localization, establishing a new state-of-the-art for the CVPR Long-Video Challenge.
